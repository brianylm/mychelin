### The Problem

Singapore's food heritage is being lost. Our pioneer and Merdeka generations hold recipes passed down orally in dialect — Hokkien, Teochew, Cantonese, Malay, Tamil — but have no way to transmit them to English-speaking grandchildren. When these seniors pass on, their recipes go with them.

### The Solution: Mychelin

I've built **Mychelin** (https://mychelin-1.vercel.app), a free digital platform for Singapore families to:

- **Record** recipes with full heritage context (dialect, region, family history, cultural occasion)
- **Capture voices** — grandma explaining her char kway teow in Hokkien, preserved forever
- **Bridge languages** — AI translation between dialects and English
- **Scan handwritten recipes** — photograph recipe cards and extract structured data

The platform is live, functional, and specifically designed for Singapore's multicultural food heritage — with categories for Hokkien, Teochew, Cantonese, Hakka, Hainanese, Nyonya, Tamil, Punjabi, and Malay traditions.
