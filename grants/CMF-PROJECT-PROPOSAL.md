# Mychelin: Preserving Singapore's Culinary Heritage Through Technology
## Project Proposal for Cultural Matching Fund (CMF) 2026

---

### 1. Executive Summary

Singapore's food heritage is disappearing. As our pioneer and Merdeka generations age, their recipes — passed down orally in dialects like Hokkien, Teochew, Cantonese, and Malay — are at risk of being lost forever. Youth dialect proficiency has plummeted, creating a language barrier between the generation that holds these recipes and the generation that needs to preserve them.

**Mychelin** is a free digital platform that enables Singapore families to capture, preserve, and share their culinary heritage. Through AI-powered language bridging (dialect ↔ English), voice recording, and structured recipe documentation, Mychelin transforms fleeting oral traditions into lasting digital heirlooms.

This project seeks CMF matching support to fund community outreach, heritage capture workshops, and continued platform development — ensuring that every Singaporean family can preserve their "Mychelin Book" of recipes, stories, and traditions.

---

### 2. Problem Statement

**Cultural loss is accelerating:**
- 25-44% of Singapore Seniors (65+) are not proficient in English (Census 2020)
- Youth dialect use has declined precipitously over two generations
- 4 in 5 isolated seniors live with family, yet nearly half report significant loneliness (Duke-NUS study)
- Heritage hawker stalls are closing at an unprecedented rate — with their recipes

**The language gap is the core barrier:**
When Ah Ma speaks only Hokkien and her grandchild speaks only English, the recipe for her signature laksa cannot be faithfully transmitted. Written recipes miss the nuances — "agak agak" (estimate), cooking by sound, smell, and feel — that make heritage food authentic.

**No existing solution addresses this:**
Recipe apps like Paprika and Recipe Keeper focus on utility (meal planning, shopping lists). They do not capture stories, record voices, bridge languages, or understand cultural context. The closest competitor, Recipe Memory (US-based, $10-20/month), has no multilingual support, no dialect capability, and no presence in Asia.

### 3. Project Description

**Mychelin is a free, open web platform (PWA) with three core capabilities:**

#### A. Heritage Recipe Capture
- Structured recipe documentation (ingredients, steps, cultural context)
- AI-powered photo scanning — photograph a handwritten recipe card and extract structured data
- Cultural taxonomy: recipes categorised by dialect group (Hokkien, Teochew, Cantonese, Hakka, Hainanese, Nyonya, Tamil, Punjabi, Malay Kampung, Eurasian)
- Occasion tagging (Chinese New Year, Hari Raya, Deepavali, family reunions)

#### B. Voice & Language Bridge
- Record Ah Ma explaining her recipe in her own dialect
- AI transcription and translation to English (and vice versa)
- Preserve the original voice — the inflections, the "agak agak", the laughter
- Bilingual recipe display (e.g. Hokkien + English side by side)

#### C. Family Story Preservation
- Rich text story fields linked to each recipe
- Cultural context: origin region, family member who passed it down, which generation
- Photo galleries: the dish, the cook, the handwritten recipe card, the family gathering

**Technical stack:** Next.js, deployed on Vercel (Singapore edge), Turso database, AI via Google Gemini. The platform is fully functional and live at https://mychelin-1.vercel.app.

### 9. Sustainability

Beyond CMF funding, Mychelin sustains through:
- **Freemium model**: Free tier for all families, premium for AI features ($4.99-9.99/month)
- **Heritage Cookbook sales**: Curated print collections by dialect group
- **Workshop facilitation fees**: Paid heritage cooking workshops for schools and corporates
- **NHB Heritage Research Grant** (July 2026 cycle): Fund AI dialect bridge R&D
- **Corporate partnerships**: F&B brands sponsoring heritage documentation

The platform itself has minimal ongoing costs (~$250/month for hosting + AI), ensuring long-term viability even without continued grant funding.
